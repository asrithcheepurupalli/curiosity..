## Observation

Someone reaches for your ribs and you flinch before they make contact. The laugh is already loading. Now try it yourself. Run your own fingers along the same ribs, the same speed, the same spot.

Nothing. A bit of pressure, maybe, but no helpless squirming, no involuntary noise.

The hand is the same hand. The nerves are the same nerves. Only the owner of the intention has changed, and somehow that is enough to switch the whole sensation off.

## Research

A tickle is partly about touch and partly about not knowing what happens next. The squirm seems to need an element of surprise, a sense that something is happening to you rather than because of you. When you tickle yourself, you supply the intention and the movement, so the surprise has nowhere to hide.

The leading explanation involves a quiet bit of bookkeeping the brain does constantly. When you move, your motor system sends a copy of the command (researchers call it an efference copy) to the sensory areas, predicting the touch your movement will produce. When the prediction matches what arrives, the brain turns the volume down. This is why you do not feel your own clothes all day, and why your own hand on your own skin registers as faint and unremarkable.

In the late 1990s, Sarah-Jayne Blakemore and colleagues tested this with a small robotic device. People moved one hand to control a soft tickler touching the other. When the device responded instantly, the sensation stayed dull. When the researchers added a fraction of a second of delay, or a slight twist in direction, the touch began to feel more ticklish again. The further it drifted from the prediction, the stronger the effect. The brain, in other words, was cancelling exactly the part it had foreseen.

This also offers a clue about why a real tickle works at all. Another person's fingers are unpredictable. Your brain cannot pre-write the script, so nothing gets cancelled, and the full strange intensity comes through. The surprise is not a side effect of the tickle. It may be the thing itself.

## Reflection

*It is a curious arrangement, to be built with a sense you are forbidden from triggering alone. The body keeps one small delight locked, and hands the key to someone else.*

*Perhaps that is the quiet lesson of the unticklish hand. Some experiences are not stored inside us waiting to be reached. They live in the gap between people, in the part we cannot predict.*
